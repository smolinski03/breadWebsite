Rosemary Focaccia

Straight from the Italy, this special recipe is perfect for a either a party or as a sunday snack. The presence of rosemary really helps to bring out the flavor!

ingredients

  1. flour
  2. active dry yeast
  3. olive oil
  4. rosemary
  5. honey

steps

  1. Proof the yeast. In the warm water and honey.
  2. Knead the dough. Gradually add in the flour, olive oil and salt. Then knead for 5 minutes using the dough attachment or a stand mixer, or knead the dough by hand.
  3. Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a greased mixing bowl, and cover with a damp towel. Let the dough rest in a warm location for 45-60 minutes, or until it has doubled in size.
  4. Shape the dough into a large circle or rectangle until that the dough is about 1/2-inch thick*. Cover and let the dough continue to rise for another 20 minutes.
  5. Heat oven to 400Â°F. Transfer the dough to a large parchment-covered baking sheet. Use your fingers to poke deep dents all over the surface of the dough. Then drizzle a tablespoon or two of olive oil evenly all over the top of the dough, and sprinkle evenly with the fresh rosemary needles and sea salt.
  6. Bake for 20 minutes, or until the dough is slightly golden and cooked through.
